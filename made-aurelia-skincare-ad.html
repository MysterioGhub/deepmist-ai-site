Production Breakdown

How we built the AURELIA ad from one still

This is a full breakdown of one 10-second ad: the brief, the opening line, the stack, and the finished video. AURELIA is a fictional brand we invented; this is a self-initiated spec ad, not client work. Skincare is one of the most skeptical categories online, so we built a test, not a claim.

Aurelia is a brand we invented. Self-initiated demo, not client work.

The finished ad. 10 seconds, 9:16, sound on. Every frame AI-generated. The “portfolio demo” watermark is ours — client deliveries ship clean.

The brief we wrote ourselves

Product
AURELIA, a fictional vitamin C face serum.
Audience
Skincare buyers who doubt before-and-after claims.
Constraint
Ten seconds, one mirror-selfie shot, no filmed footage.
Format
Mirror-selfie UGC · talking head
Niche
Skincare

The opening line

“One month, no filter, no foundation.”

The opening line is 'One month, no filter, no foundation.' In the first second it names what a skeptical skincare viewer is already checking for — filters and foundation covering the skin. It sets a testable frame the viewer can watch for, not a claim they would dismiss.

Why this format. Skincare trust is personal, so a single face talking to camera carries more weight than a product beauty shot. The mirror-selfie framing borrows the look of a real user review, which is the format this audience already believes.

How it was built

  1. 01

    Hero still

    One image, generated with Nano Banana Pro at 9:16. Framed like a phone photo rather than a product shot: the item in hand, the brand name legible on the label, light coming from somewhere plausible.

  2. 02

    Motion and voice

    That still becomes frame one. Seedance 2.0 takes it to ten seconds of 720p video and generates the audio in the same pass. Spoken lines are written into the prompt, so the delivery is lip-synced from the start instead of dubbed on afterwards.

  3. 03

    Check before keep

    Nothing survives because it looked fine as a thumbnail. We read the stream data with ffprobe, build a contact sheet of frames with ffmpeg, and compare the spoken audio against the script that was written. Takes that drift from the brief get regenerated, not patched.

  4. 04

    Finish

    The approved take is cut and packaged with ffmpeg for wherever it will run. Vertical for Reels and TikTok here; the same take can be reframed for feed or landscape if the brief asks.

What to watch for

The spoken line

The words are spoken by the person and lip-synced in the same render pass, not dubbed over the footage afterwards.

The bottle label

The serum bottle is framed close and the AURELIA name sits on the label, so the product reads without a graphic overlay.

The handheld framing

The shot is built as a mirror selfie with a slight handheld sway, the imperfection that makes a clip feel filmed rather than generated.
