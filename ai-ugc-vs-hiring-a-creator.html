When to hire a human creator

Real testimony

When the ad needs a genuine personal story only a real user can tell, a human who actually lives with your product carries weight a synthetic presenter cannot.

The physical product

Showing real texture, fit, taste or assembly needs an actual body and hands. Fabric on skin, food being eaten, a tool being built. AI still fakes these poorly.

Their own audience

When you want the video posted from a creator's own handle to followers who already trust them, only a real account with a real following can do that.

Trust-sensitive categories

In categories where buyers punish anything that smells synthetic (wellness, finance, baby care, medical), a real human face lowers the risk the ad is dismissed as fake.

When AI production is the better buy

Hook testing at volume

When you need many variations to find the winning hook, AI makes each extra variation a cheap render rather than another shoot, fee and shipping round.

No sample logistics

You never ship a product, wait on a creator's calendar, or risk a wasted sample. We work from the photos you already have, so nothing physical moves.

Rights that never expire

Because no real person's likeness is licensed, full usage rights come with the pack and do not expire. You can run the ad for as long as it works.

Hard-to-source markets

When you need a presenter for a language or market where you cannot easily find and vet a local creator, AI lets you produce there without the sourcing work.

Disclosure, both ways

Every ad we deliver is disclosed as AI wherever it runs, and you should do the same. TikTok requires an AI disclosure tag on ads using synthetic faces or voices, and Meta requires a label on paid content showing AI-generated people. Clear disclosure reads as confidence, not weakness.
