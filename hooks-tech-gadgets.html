How to use one

What works in tech

One problem, named

Tech hooks die when they list features. Name the single annoyance the gadget kills, like a battery dead by noon or a desk buried in cables.

Show the before

Buyers believe a fix only after they have seen the mess. Open on the cluttered desk or the dying phone, then reveal the gadget doing its one job.

Price as proof

A dollar figure does real work in tech. Lead with the cost of the fix, not the feature list, and the hook reads as a recommendation.

Use these honestly

These are scripted ad openers — the same way any commercial is scripted. They are written to be adapted to something true about your product, not to invent something that isn't.

Keep them clean: don't bolt on fabricated numbers, invented review counts, fake scarcity, or results you can't stand behind. A hook earns the next three seconds. Your product has to earn the rest.
